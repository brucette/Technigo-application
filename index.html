Scrum

Scrum is a project management methodology, a framework for how a team should work together towards a common goal like delivering a product or service.

The Scrum Team has no hierarchies and consists of:

  • Scrum Master - ensures the team follows the scrum methodology.

  • Product Owner - maintains and helps prioritise the project's to-do-list.

  • Development team - self-managing group of ~ 7 members, gets the work done.


Scrum is characterised by short phases (~ 2 - 4 weeks) called sprints, in which project work occurs.

A sprint consists of:

  • Sprint Planning - the team plans what work is to be performed and sets the sprint goal.

  • Daily Scrum/Stand-up - a short daily meeting to make sure everyone is aligned with the sprint goal and to voice any potential concerns.

  • Sprint Review - the team inspects and demonstrates the completed work to stakeholders for feedback.

  • Sprint Retrospective - the team documents and discusses what went well and what needs improvement for the next sprint.

This cycle is repeated until the entirety of the project has been delivered.
